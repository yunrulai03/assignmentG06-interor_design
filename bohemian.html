Bohemian Style

‘Bohemian interior design offers a blend of creativity, comfort, and artistic expression in each room ’

Bohemian design, also known as boho design, is a style of interior design that revels in playful colors, textures, and patterns. Unlike minimalist Scandinavian or modern design, boho design embraces maximalism and encourages individual expression through the curation and layering of multicultural textiles, art works, and decorative objects. Bohemian interior design embraces decor ideas from around the globe. Its free-spirit, anything-goes approach made it one of the most popular interior design trends of the late twentieth and early twenty-first century.


1.Living Room


The Bohemian style of interior design is known for its eclectic and artistic flair. In a Bohemian living room, you can expect a mix of colors, patterns, and textures that create a vibrant and inviting space. One key aspect of a Bohemian living room is comfortable and cozy seating arrangements. Low sofas or floor cushions adorned with colorful and patterned textiles create a relaxed and laid-back atmosphere. Incorporating vintage furniture and unique pieces from various cultures adds character and charm to the space. In terms of decor, you'll find an assortment of plants, art pieces, tapestries, and eclectic accessories that reflect the owner's free-spirited and adventurous personality.

2. Master Bedroom

A Bohemian-style master bedroom offers a cozy and intimate sanctuary that combines comfort with artistic expression. The bed serves as the focal point and is often adorned with layers of plush bedding, such as soft quilts, throw blankets, and a mix of patterned and embroidered pillows. Canopies or drapes made of sheer fabrics add a dreamy and romantic touch. Bohemian bedrooms embrace an abundance of natural light, so lightweight curtains or blinds allow the sun to filter through. The use of warm, earthy tones and rich jewel colors, combined with soft lighting, creates a soothing and intimate ambiance. Unique and vintage furniture pieces, such as distressed nightstands or a vintage vanity, further enhance the Bohemian vibe.


3. Children Room

A Bohemian-style children's room is a playful and imaginative space that encourages creativity and exploration. Colors and patterns take center stage, with a mix of vibrant hues and whimsical designs. Wall murals or wallpaper featuring nature scenes or abstract patterns can set the stage for a magical environment. Low seating options, such as bean bags or floor cushions, provide a comfortable and relaxed area for play and reading. Shelves filled with books, art supplies, and toys spark the child's imagination. Incorporating a variety of textures, such as woven rugs, plush toys, and tactile wall hangings, adds depth and sensory stimulation to the room.


4. Bathroom

While Bohemian style is often associated with the communal spaces of a home, it can also be reflected in smaller areas like the bathroom. Even in this space, you can infuse elements of Bohemian design to create an interesting and visually appealing environment. Consider using patterned tiles or textured wallpaper to add a touch of Bohemian flair to the walls. Vintage-inspired fixtures and accessories, such as decorative mirrors, ornate sconces, and colorful accents, can inject personality into the space. Adding potted plants or hanging planters brings a natural and fresh element to the bathroom.


5. Kitchen

A Bohemian-style kitchen combines functionality with artistic charm. Natural materials like wood and stone are often incorporated into countertops, cabinets, and flooring. Open shelving can display a mix of colorful ceramic dishes, glassware, and cookware, creating a visually captivating arrangement. Bohemian kitchens often feature bold and vibrant colors, both in the form of painted cabinets or accent walls. Natural light is emphasized, so large windows or skylights are desirable. Hanging plants or herbs can provide a touch of greenery and a fresh vibe to the space.


Common elements
  • Layered textiles and textures : A hallmark of bohemian style is the abundant use of textiles. Layered rugs, tapestries, throws, and pillows in various textures and patterns create a cozy and inviting atmosphere.
  • Plants and greenery : Bohemian interiors tend to incorporate an abundance of plants. Hanging planters, potted plants, and cascading vines bring life and a sense of nature indoors.
  • Vintage and salvaged furniture : Mixing old and new is common in bohemian style. Vintage or antique furniture pieces with character, like a distressed wooden dresser or a retro armchair, can be paired with more modern elements.
Common Colours
  • Electic mix of colors and patterns : Bohemian rooms often feature vibrant and rich colors, such as jewel tones, mixed with earthy hues. Patterns like paisley, ikat, and ethnic prints are commonly incorporated.
