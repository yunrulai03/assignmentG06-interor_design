Industrial Style

‘Industrial style interior design draws inspiration from old factories and industrial spaces, combining raw and unfinished elements with sleek and modern aesthetics. It embraces a utilitarian and edgy vibe, showcasing exposed brick walls, metal accents, and a neutral color palette. ’

As the name implies, “industrial interior design is inspired by industrial buildings and elements such as exposed pipes, metal, concrete, and brick,” explains Del Piero. “Industrial spaces typically include open spaces with a wealth of windows and natural light.” Because of these elements, the design style typically includes a darker—though neutral—color palette such as white, black, and gray. “Using materials in its rawest and roughest forms, elevated by black and sleek detailing, you can curate an industrial style environment as we know it today,” add Truong and Dang. “It often embodies minimalism, playing on the movement of natural and raw materials to set the tone, and uses dark metals to tie the look together.”


1.Living Room


The living room is an ideal space to incorporate industrial design elements. Start by exposing the brick walls if possible, as they serve as a key feature in industrial aesthetics. Leave the structural beams and ductwork exposed to emphasize the raw and industrial look. Opt for furniture with clean lines and a mix of materials like leather, metal, and distressed wood. Incorporate vintage or repurposed items such as an old factory cart as a coffee table or metal shelving for displaying decor pieces. Enhance the space with industrial lighting fixtures, such as pendant lights or exposed bulb chandeliers, to add an urban ambiance.

2. Master Bedroom

For the master bedroom, industrial style can create a chic and sophisticated retreat. Use a neutral color palette with shades of gray, black, and white as the base. Combine these with warm wooden accents to add a touch of warmth. Choose a platform bed with a metal frame and rustic wooden nightstands. Integrate industrial-inspired lighting, such as wall sconces with exposed bulbs or metal pendant lights. Incorporate vintage elements like an antique chest or an industrial-style dresser to add character to the room.


3. Children Room

In an industrial-inspired children's room, you can create a playful and imaginative space while maintaining the overall aesthetic. Use a neutral color palette with pops of vibrant colors for accents. Incorporate industrial-inspired furniture, such as a metal bed frame or a metal desk with a wooden top. Add industrial-style storage solutions, like metal lockers or wire baskets, to keep toys organized. Use wall art featuring transportation themes or vintage posters to enhance the industrial vibe.

4. Bathroom

The industrial style can be applied to the toilet by focusing on a minimalistic and utilitarian approach. Choose materials like concrete or subway tiles for the walls and floors. Incorporate a metal vanity with a stainless steel countertop and exposed plumbing fixtures. Add an industrial-style mirror with a distressed metal frame and vintage-inspired lighting fixtures to complete the look. Use minimalistic accessories and keep the space clutter-free to maintain the industrial aesthetic.

5. Kitchen

In the kitchen, industrial style can create a sleek and modern space. Opt for stainless steel appliances and concrete or stainless steel countertops. Expose the brick walls if possible, or use brick-like tiles for a similar effect. Incorporate open shelving with metal brackets to display dishware and add a touch of industrial charm. Industrial-style pendant lights above the kitchen island or dining table can serve as statement pieces. Use metal bar stools with distressed finishes to complement the overall design.

Industrial's common element and colour :

Common elements Description
Utilitarian furniture Idustrial-style rooms favor furniture with a utilitarian and functional appeal. Pieces made of metal, reclaimed wood, or vintage industrial materials, such as metal-framed tables, distressed leather sofas, or industrial-style stools, are commonly used.
Raw and distressed materials Industrial design embraces raw and distressed materials. This includes unfinished wood, worn leather, and aged metals. These materials bring a sense of authenticity and character to the space.
Open floor plans and spacious layouts Industrial-style rooms often have open floor plans, with minimal partitioning walls. This layout allows for a sense of spaciousness and highlights the industrial features of the space.
Industrial lighting fixtures Industrial-style rooms incorporate lighting fixtures reminiscent of factory or warehouse lighting. Pendant lights with metal shades, exposed bulbs, and vintage-inspired fixtures are commonly used to enhance the industrial atmosphere.


Common colours Description
Neutral color palette with pops of metal Industrial design typically incorporates a neutral color scheme, including shades of gray, black, and white. Accents of metal, such as steel, iron, or copper, are used to add contrast and visual interest.
