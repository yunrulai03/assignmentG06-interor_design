Chinese Style

‘China has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its traditional style of interior design. Traditional Chinese style is known for its use of bold colors, intricate details, and decorative elements that symbolize good fortune and prosperity. ’

The Chinese style is one of the ancient arts, originating in a civilization considered one of the oldest in the world, dating back to about five thousand years BC. This decoration is still popular in the market for many reasons, including that it is comfortable to look at on the one hand, and gives the place spaciousness and spaciousness on the other hand. One of the most important features of Chinese decoration is that it is characterized by creativity and sophistication, as its lines contributed to adding the distinctive features of each of its ancient and modern eras. It is characterized by simplicity, calmness and harmonious colors. Each piece has its own distinctive personality, and thus its location that suggests elegance and sophistication.


1. Master bedroom

In traditional Chinese style interior design, the master bedroom is often the most important room in the home, as it is believed to represent the overall well-being and success of the household. The furniture used in the master bedroom is carefully chosen to promote a sense of calm, balance, and harmony while also incorporating the principles of Feng Shui and Wu Xing.

One of the most important pieces of furniture in the master bedroom is the bed. In Feng Shui principles, the bed should be positioned in a way that allows for a clear view of the door and ample space around the bed for good energy flow. The bed should also have a solid headboard, which is believed to provide support and stability in one's life. Traditional Chinese style beds are often made from natural materials such as wood, and may be decorated with carvings or other intricate designs.

Another important piece of furniture in the master bedroom is the wardrobe or closet. In traditional Chinese style, the wardrobe is often made from natural materials and is designed to provide ample storage space while also fitting seamlessly into the overall design of the room. In Feng Shui principles, it is recommended to keep the closet organized and clutter- free to promote good energy flow.

Additionally, traditional Chinese style master bedrooms often include seating areas, such as a pair of armchairs or a small sofa. These areas are designed for relaxation and conversation, and can be used for reading or enjoying a cup of tea. In Feng Shui principles, it is recommended to have a clear view of the door from the seating area to promote a sense of security and well-being.

In terms of the five elements of Wu Xing, wood and earth are commonly used in traditional Chinese style interior design for the master bedroom. The use of natural materials such as wood and bamboo is believed to promote a sense of calm and balance in the space, while earthy tones such as beige and brown can promote grounding and stability.

The furniture used in traditional Chinese style master bedroom design is carefully chosen to promote a sense of calm, balance, and harmony while also incorporating the principles of Feng Shui and Wu Xing. By using natural materials, muted colors, and careful placement of furniture, you can create a space that promotes restful sleep, relaxation, and overall well-being.


2. Living Room

In traditional Chinese style interior design, the living room is often a central gathering place for family and friends, and is designed to promote harmony and balance while incorporating the principles of Feng Shui and Wu Xing. The furniture used in the living room is carefully chosen to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere while also promoting good energy flow.

  1. Seating area. Sofas and armchairs are often made from natural materials such as wood or rattan, and may be decorated with intricate carvings or patterns. In Feng Shui principles, it is recommended to position the seating area so that it faces the door and allows for clear communication and conversation.
  2. Sofas and chairs. Often made from natural materials such as wood, rattan, or bamboo, and may be decorated with carvings or other intricate designs. Additionally, seating may be complemented by a coffee table or other small tables, which can be used for holding drinks or other items.
  3. Coffee table. These tables are often made from wood or other natural materials and may feature intricate designs or carvings. In Feng Shui principles, it is recommended to choose a coffee table that is round or oval in shape, as these shapes promote good energy flow and harmony in the space.
  4. Screens or room dividers. These screens may feature intricate designs or patterns, and can be used to create separate spaces within the larger room. In Feng Shui principles, it is recommended to choose screens that are made from natural materials and that feature auspicious symbols or patterns.

3. Kitchen

Early 20th century kitchen cabinet. Kitchen cabinets were an important part of the traditional Chinese home. Dishes prepared by the family cook would be placed in the cabinet ahead of being served. Anything left over could be returned to the cabinet. The doors have open lattice in the form of a long life motif with a calligraphy medallion. The freely sliding lattice panels on the lower section were used for stacking freshly washed cookware.

The kitchen is by far the place we spend the most time in our homes. Whether the heart of the family home, the home cook’s getaway, or the room at a party where everyone always gathers, we all want a great kitchen! Be it in a big family home or a cozy loft apartment, our kitchen is our oasis.

  1. Kitchen Components : Let’s think about some of the key elements of a Chinese kitchen: We have the appliances – fridge, freezer, dishwasher, cooker, air-conditioning units, ventilators, etc. Then there are the cabinets, work-tops, sinks etc. In addition, the table and chairs, flooring, islands…
  2. Space : You have the opportunity to make the layout of the kitchen work with your space; for example, L-shaped, U-shaped, parallel, or surrounding an island. If your kitchen is small, as can be the case with Chinese kitchens in the city, you can consider draws, freestanding cabinets or wall-hung cupboards and units for great storage space saving solutions.
  • Rich and vibrant colors : Chinese-style rooms often feature a rich and bold color palette. Red, gold, black, and shades of blue are commonly used to symbolize prosperity, luck, and happiness. These colors are incorporated through wall paint, furniture, and decorative accents.
Common elements
  • Oriental motifs and patterns : Traditional Chinese motifs and patterns are frequently used in Chinese-style rooms. Examples include intricate floral designs, dragons, phoenixes, clouds, and other symbolic elements. These patterns can be found on wallpaper, upholstery, rugs, or decorative pieces.
  • Separating Screens : Used for both decorative and room-dividing purposes, screens are commonplace in Chinese interior design and Oriental Casa has many options for you to choose from. Folding screens adorned with detailed murals are often mythological or historical in theme.
  • Lattice Work : Chinese design reflects a respect for detail and precision in the decorative pieces created. Beautiful latticework is often seen incorporated into cabinet doors and furniture trim. Many of our beds have latticework on the frames, with patterns being geometric and/or floral-based.
